Enchilada Casserole

This enchilada casserole is a delicious and easy-to-make dish that is perfect for a weeknight dinner or a weekend gathering. The casserole is layered with seasoned ground beef, melted cheese, and a rich tomato sauce, creating a hearty and flavorful meal that is sure to please everyone at the table. Serve it with a side of rice and beans, and a fresh salad for a complete and satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 can (10 ounces) enchilada sauce
  • 1 can (4 ounces) diced green chilies
  • 1 cup shredded Cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup shredded Monterey Jack cheese
  • 8 small flour tortillas
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 cup sliced jalapeños (optional)

Instructions

  2. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  3. In a large skillet, cook ground beef, onion, and garlic over medium heat until meat is no longer pink; drain excess fat.
  4. Stir in enchilada sauce and diced green chilies; simmer for 5 minutes.
  5. Spread 1/2 cup of the meat sauce in the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  6. Layer 4 tortillas over the sauce, overlapping slightly.
  7. Spread 1/2 cup of the meat sauce over each tortilla layer.
  8. Sprinkle 1/2 cup of each cheese over each layer.
  9. Repeat layers, ending with a layer of tortillas and cheese on top.
  10. Pour remaining meat sauce over top of casserole.
  11. Bake in preheated oven for 25 minutes, or until cheese is melted and bubbly.

Garnish with chopped fresh cilantro and sliced jalapeños, if desired. Serve hot and enjoy your delicious enchilada casserole!
